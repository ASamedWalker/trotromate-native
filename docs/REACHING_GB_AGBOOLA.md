# Reaching Olugbenga "GB" Agboola — Playbook

## Who He Is

- **Founder & CEO, Flutterwave** — Africa's payments infrastructure ($3B+ valuation)
- **Background:** Electrical Engineering (Obafemi Awolowo), MBA (Oxford Said), exec programs at MIT Sloan. Previously at PayPal, Google Wallet, Standard Bank.
- **Recognition:** Fortune 40 Under 40, Time Next 100, Nigerian National Honour (OON)
- Co-founded Flutterwave in 2016. Sole CEO since 2018.

## Why He Matters for Troski

Flutterwave already has:
- **Enhanced PSP license from Bank of Ghana** (August 2024) — highest tier
- **Inward remittance approval** in Ghana (2025)
- **MTN MoMo, Vodafone Cash, Airtel Money** integration in Ghana
- **Live developer APIs** for GHS transactions

There is **no existing Flutterwave transport vertical**. Troski would be one of the first high-frequency transit payment merchants on their Ghana infrastructure. Trotro rides are low-value but extremely frequent — exactly the transaction profile that scales payment processor revenue.

**The precedent:** Flutterwave helped Uber solve their Lagos card processing problem — that was an early credibility-defining partnership. Troski is the informal, mass-market version of that same opportunity.

## What He's Said About What Impresses Him

From interviews on The Flip, McKinsey, Wharton FinTech, Medium (Microtraction):

1. **"Put your customers at the center."** He credits YC with this. Show real user data and real pain, not slide decks.
2. **"Stay true to your path."** He's unimpressed by opportunistic pivots. Wants missionaries, not mercenaries.
3. **"Build for the African opportunity at scale."** A niche in one country isn't VC-fundable. Frame Troski as "the payments layer for informal transit across West Africa."
4. **"Partnerships are vital."** He respects founders who understand their startup can be a distribution channel for larger infrastructure.
5. **"Customer-defined expansion."** Flutterwave only expanded when customers pulled them. "Our users already use MTN MoMo via Flutterwave — we want to build this integration properly" speaks his language.

## Step-by-Step Playbook

### Step 1 — Build a Warm Intro

Cold outreach to a $3B CEO has low odds. Priority order:
1. Someone in Flutterwave's **Ghana business development team** (search LinkedIn)
2. A **Resilience17 portfolio founder** (Klasha, Brass, Bamboo founders)
3. Someone in the **YC Ghana/West Africa network** (he's a YC alum)
4. A mutual in the **MIT Africa community** (he's speaking at AIC 2025)
5. A mutual in the **Milken Institute Africa Leaders Council**

### Step 2 — Become a Flutterwave Merchant First

Integrate Flutterwave's API into Troski for fare payments. Become a live merchant on their Ghana platform with real transaction volume. Then outreach isn't a cold pitch — it's a merchant with traction asking for a commercial conversation. Their sales team will listen, and the path to GB becomes internal escalation.

### Step 3 — Apply to Go Time AI (May 2025)

If Troski has any AI component (route prediction, demand forecasting, driver earnings optimization), apply. $25K-$175K for 8% with direct access to GB's team. Website: [resilience17.com](https://resilience17.com)

### Step 4 — Attend MIT Africa Innovation Conference (AIC 2025)

GB is a listed speaker. The conference is built for African founders and is accessible to attendees. Come with one sharp question that demonstrates you understand the payments infrastructure gap in informal transit. Don't pitch at the event — ask a question that makes him curious about you.

### Step 5 — Engage on X with Substance

Not "great tweet, big fan." Find posts about African fintech or Ghana payments. Reply with specific insights from trotro digitization:

> *"We're seeing 60-80% of our Accra commuters have MTN MoMo but zero transit apps accept it. The infrastructure exists, the behaviour change problem is what we're solving."*

Do this 3-4 times over several weeks before any DM.

### Step 6 — Frame the Pitch

Do NOT lead with "we want investment." Lead with:

> *"Troski is live in Accra processing [X] trips/day. We're already using mobile money. We want to build the reference case for informal transit payments on Flutterwave's infrastructure across West Africa."*

Show the transaction volume opportunity:
- [daily trotro trips in Accra] x average fare x payment processing fee = TAM for a processor
- Frame Troski as the merchant that proves informal transit payments in Ghana, replicable across Lagos danfos, Nairobi matatus, Dakar car rapides
- Mention that Uber was his early proof point in mobility — Troski is the mass-market version

### Step 7 — Direct DM (Last Resort)

Twitter DM (@TechProd_Arch) is more likely read by him personally than email. Keep to 3 sentences: who you are, one proof point, and a single clear ask (15-minute call). No attachments, no deck.

---

## The Key Insight

For Flutterwave, Troski is not a charity case — it's a **high-volume merchant opportunity**. 3.5 million daily trotro trips in Accra alone, all currently cash. Even capturing 5% of that digitally through Flutterwave's rails generates significant transaction fee revenue. Frame every conversation around this mutual commercial value.
